## Takeaways

- TDD is a design tool; tests are executable specs
- Red-Green-Refactor is a feedback loop, not ceremony
- Real payoff is compounding: safer refactoring, faster debugging, clearer intent

---

## Practical Starting Point

Next time you add a context hook or utility, write one failing test first.

Keep scope narrow. Learn the loop on one behavior.

---

## Team Challenge

Pick one upcoming ticket.
Try Inside Out TDD on the business logic layer first.
Share what changed in debugging speed and confidence.

---

## Final Message

The biggest shift for me was this:

I stopped asking, "Does it look right right now?"

And started asking, "Have I defined the behavior clearly enough that the code can prove it?"
